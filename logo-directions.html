JRNL Logo Directions

Five marks drawn from the app's own world — anthracite, a single high-altitude orange, condensed display type. Each is shown as a standalone mark, a horizontal lockup, and at app-icon / favicon scale to confirm it survives shrinking. No new colors or styles introduced.

Recommendation

Ship the Cairn. A cairn is the stacked-stone marker climbers and trail runners build to mark a route where there's no path — which is exactly what a training journal is: the markers you leave to find your way back and forward. It's conceptually precise, unmistakably mountain without a literal peak (which every outdoor brand uses), and it reduces cleanly to three stacked blocks at favicon size.

If you want something flatter and more system-like, the Gain bars are the safe alt — they echo the weekly-load chart already in the app, tying the logo directly to the product. Keep the current diamond only as a fallback.
